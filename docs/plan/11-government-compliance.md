# 11 — Government Compliance (Philippines)

## Overview

Running a used cooking oil collection business in the Philippines requires coordination with several government agencies. The good news is that used cooking oil is classified as hazardous waste — which means there's already a regulatory framework for what you're doing. You're not breaking new ground; you're formalizing an existing industry.

## Key Legal Framework

| Law | What It Covers | Relevance to OilTrace |
|-----|---------------|----------------------|
| **RA 6969** | Toxic Substances and Hazardous and Nuclear Wastes Control Act of 1990 | Used cooking oil = hazardous waste. Collection/transport/processing requires DENR permits. |
| **DAO 2013-22** (DENR) | Revised procedures for hazardous waste management | Classifies used cooking oil as hazardous waste. Defines TSD (Treatment, Storage, Disposal) requirements. |
| **RA 7394** | Consumer Act of the Philippines | Points system must be transparent. DTI oversight for promotions. |
| **RA 10173** | Data Privacy Act of 2012 | Consumer data collection requires NPC registration. |
| **RA 9367** | Biofuels Act of 2006 | Mandates biodiesel blend in diesel. Creates demand for biofuel feedstock (your oil). |

## Why Compliance Is Actually Good for You

Used cooking oil in the PH is currently:

1. **Dumped down drains** — causes flooding, environmental damage
2. **Re-sold illegally** — unscrupulous collectors sell it back as cooking oil (health hazard)
3. **Unregulated** — most karinderyas have no disposal option

Your service solves all three problems. LGUs are actively passing ordinances that:
- **Require** establishments to use accredited collectors
- **Penalize** illegal dumping and re-selling (₱2,000-5,000 fines)
- **Support** formal collection programs

Your DENR permits and LGU accreditation are **competitive moats** — not just compliance paperwork.

## Key Insight for Hackathon Judges

When you pitch this project, say:

> *"Used cooking oil is classified as hazardous waste under RA 6969. Most karinderyas don't know this and dump oil illegally. OilTrace provides a formal, compliant, and incentivized collection channel — with blockchain traceability to prove every liter was disposed of properly."*

This shows you understand the regulatory landscape, which is a strong signal to judges.
